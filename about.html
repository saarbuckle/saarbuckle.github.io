About me

My interest in touch and motor control—a central focus of my research—stems from my enthusiasm for rock climbing and outdoor recreation. More broadly, I'm passionate about all things science and data, including infrastructures that enable (and grow) science and research, the use of science and data in policy making, and the advacement of data literacy.

I grew up in Winnipeg.

Education

I completed a BSc in Psychology at Queen's University where I worked with Dr. Ronald Holden and completed a thesis with Dr. Ingrid Johnsrude. I then worked with Dr. Ingrid Johnsrude and Dr. Stefan Köhler as a research assistant. I completed a PhD in Neuroscience with Dr. Jörn Diedrichsen and Dr. Andrew Pruszynski at Western University. I was funded by NSERC and some of my projects have received funding from BrainsCAN.

Teaching

I've helped design and teach courses in data science, neuroimaging, statistics, and psychology (through this, I too get to learn about cool topics!). I am currently collaborating with a team at the Centre for Neuroscience Studies (Queen's University) to create a series of short courses on neurotechnology. These courses are designed to be accessible to learners of all backgrounds and will highlight how neurotechnologies can help tackle real-world problems.
