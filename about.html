Personal: I am a neuroscientist passionate about movement. This passion is rooted in my personal life, where I am an avid rock climber. I grew up in Winnipeg.

Education: I completed a BSc in Psychology at Queen's University where I was completed a thesis under the supervision of Ingrid Johnsrude. During this time, I also worked with Ronald Holden. After my undergrad- and prior to starting grad school- I worked with Ingrid Johnsrude and Stefan Köhler at Western University. I completed my PhD with Jörn Diedrichsen and Andrew Pruszynski at Western University. I was funded by NSERC and some of my projects received research funding from BrainsCAN.

Teaching: I have 10+ years of teaching related experience. I've helped design and teach courses in data science, neuroimaging, statistics, and psychology. I am currently collaborating with a team at the Centre for Neuroscience Studies (Queen's University) to create a series of short courses on neurotechnology (more info here). These courses are designed to be accessible to learners of all backgrounds and will highlight how we can use neurotechnologies beyond the lab to help tackle real-world problems.
