About me

I am an avid rock climber. This passion has spurred my interest in touch and motor control—part of my research focus.

My interest and enthusiatism extends to include all sorts of science-related topics, including infrastructures that enable (and grow) science and research, the use of science in policy making, and the advacement of scientific literacy.

I grew up in Winnipeg.

Education

I completed a BSc in Psychology at Queen's University where I was supervised by Dr. Ingrid Johnsrude for my honours thesis project. During this time, I also worked with Dr. Ronald Holden. After my undergrad, I worked with Dr. Ingrid Johnsrude and Dr. Stefan Köhler as a research assistant. I then completed a PhD in Neuroscience with Dr. Jörn Diedrichsen and Dr. Andrew Pruszynski at Western University. I was funded by NSERC and some of my projects received research funding from BrainsCAN.

Teaching

I've helped design and teach courses in data science, neuroimaging, statistics, and psychology (through this, I too get to learn about cool topics!). I am currently collaborating with a team at the Centre for Neuroscience Studies (Queen's University) to create a series of short courses on neurotechnology. These courses are designed to be accessible to learners of all backgrounds and will highlight how neurotechnologies can help tackle real-world problems.
