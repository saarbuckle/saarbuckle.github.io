I am a neuroscientist passionate about movement. This passion is rooted in my personal life, where I am an avid climber.

I am from Winnipeg (Manitoba, Canada). I completed a BSc in Psychology at Queen's University (Kingston, Ontario) where I was supervised by Ingrid Johnsrude for my honours thesis project. During this time I also worked with Ronald Holden. After my undergrad (and prior to starting grad school), I worked with Ingrid Johnsrude and Stefan Köhler at Western University (London, Ontario).

I completed my PhD with Jörn Diedrichsen and Andrew Pruszynski at Western University. I was funded by NSERC and some of my projects were supported by BrainsCAN.

I have helped teach courses in data science, neuroimaging, statistics, and psychology.
