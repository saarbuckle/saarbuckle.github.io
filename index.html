I have a knack for figuring out what the thorny problems are and devising data-driven ways to solve them.

My collaborations include:

  • MindMaze, where we use digital theraputics for restoration;
  • Statistics Canada, where we used alternative data (any non-survey data, like geospatial and web scraped data) to measure public health;
  • The Canadian Science Policy Centre, a science and innovation policy hub;
  • Queen's University, where we developed a series of neurotechnology courses;
  • Western University, where we developed models and conducted experiments to study how we sense and move.
