Skillful hand movements- like writing or playing the piano- are a defining human ability. These dexterous movements, which involve fine fractionated control of individual fingers, require interplay between sensorimotor and cognitive processes. Although this might sound demanding, when we use our hands it often feels effortless.

I'm interested in how the control of dexterous movements is distributed and coordinated across the sensorimotor network. How is it that these processes operate so seamlessly? How is it that these movements can feel so automatic? In my research, I combine computational models with measures of brain and muscle activity to study what underlying principles might govern hand control. My intent is that this work can be used to help define benchmarks when assessing patients who present with impaired hand function.
