I have a knack for figuring out what the thorny problems are and devising data-driven ways to solve them.

My collaborations include:

  • MindMaze, where we use digital theraputics for neurological restoration;
  • Statistics Canada, where we used alternative (non-survey data, such as geospatial and transactional data) data to monitor public health;
  • Canadian Science Policy Centre, a science and innovation policy hub;
  • Queen's University, where we created a series of neurotechnology primers;
  • Western University, where we worked to better grasp hand control.
