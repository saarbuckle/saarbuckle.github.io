Dexterous hand movements- like writing or playing the piano- are a defining human ability, and require interplay between sensorimotor control and cognitive processes. Although this might sound demanding, when we use our hands it often feels effortless. Why is this?

I am interested in how we control our hands with relative ease, how hand use influences cognition, and (more broadly) what are dexterous movements? To study such questions, I build and test models that predict how features of hand control might be encoded in patterns of brain-activity.
