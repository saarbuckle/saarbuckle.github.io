I am a neuroscientist who studies how the nervous system controls movement.

Skillful hand movements- like writing or playing the piano- are a defining human ability. These movements, which involve fine fractionated control of individual fingers, require interplay between sensorimotor and cognitive processes. Although this might sound demanding, when we use our hands it often feels effortless.

How is it that these hand movements can feel so automatic? And how do we process sensory information from the skin when we are engaged in such movements? In my research, I address these questions using computational models combined with measures of brain, muscle, and behavioural activity.
