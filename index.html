Skillful hand movements- like writing or playing the piano- are a defining human ability. These dexterous movements, which involve fine fractionated control of individual fingers, require interplay between sensorimotor and cognitive processes. Although this might sound demanding, when we use our hands it often feels effortless. How is it that these processes operate so seamlessly?

In my research, I study the sensorimotor processes that facilitate dexterous hand movements. I'm interested in the sensorimotor processes that facilitate dexterous hand movements and how we process tactile information from the hand. More broadly, I'm interested in what are the underlying principles of dexterous movements, and how hand use influences cognition (& vice versa). To study such questions, I build and test models that predict how features of hand control might be encoded in patterns of brain and muscle activity.
