Hands are complex. How are we able to control them with relative easily? In my research, I use functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) and neural recordings to study how neurons in the brain function together to help control hand movements.

Currently, I am a senior Neuroscience PhD student co-supervised by Dr. Jörn Diedrichsen and Dr. Andrew Pruszynski at The Brain and Mind Institute, University of Western Ontario, London (ON), Canada.
