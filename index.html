About

I am interested in how primates are able to perform highly complex hand movements. Skillful hand use reflects intelligent integration of tactile sensory information with motor plans. In my work, I have focused on the role of the primary sensorimotor cortices in hand control: both from the perspectives of motor control and tactile sensory integration. To this end, I use functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) and neural recordings.

Currently, I am a senior Neuroscience PhD candidate co-supervised by Dr. Jörn Diedrichsen and Dr. Andrew Pruszynski at The Brain and Mind Institute, University of Western Ontario, London (ON), Canada.
